Calasiao

Calasiao was founded by the Dominicans in 1588. The town's name is derived from the root word "Lasi", which means a place of lightning. It is renowned for its delicious Puto (rice cakes) and is home to the NCCA-acknowledged cultural treasure, the Sts. Peter and Paul Parish Church.
