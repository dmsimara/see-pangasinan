Explore the Cultural Heritage of Pangasinan

Banáan Pangasinan Provincial Museum

The Banáan Pangasinan Provincial Museum, situated in the historic Casa Real in Lingayen, serves as a vital repository of the province’s cultural heritage. The museum features eleven galleries that depict Pangasinan’s history, culture, arts, and innovations. Notably, the Asin Gallery hosts changing exhibits that showcase local artists and significant historical themes.

Jose Palma Historical Marker

In 1899, Jose Palma wrote the poem “Filipinas” in Bautista, Pangasinan. This poem later became the lyrics of the Philippine national anthem. In 2015, the National Historical Commission of the Philippines (NHCP) recognized this site with a historical marker, which was unveiled in 2017 alongside a statue of Jose Palma.

Giant Farmer and Carabao Statue

Built in 2006 in front of the town plaza of Bugallon, this monument features giant figures of a farmer and a carabao. Created by the local artist group Anak Pawis, this statue commemorates the town’s agricultural heritage, reminding passersby of the significance of farming in Bugallon’s history.

Metamorphosis Monument

The Metamorphosis Monument in Urdaneta City, unveiled in 2011, is a bronze statue of a carabao, symbolizing the city’s official seal. Sculpted by Ram Mamalio, this artwork features a cascading man-made waterfall and granite backdrop, reflecting Urdaneta’s cultural and historical identity.

Bantayog-Wika ng Pangasinan

In 2018, the Komisyon sa Wikang Filipino (KWF) launched the Bantayog-Wika project to honor major Philippine languages, including Pangasinan. The language monument, located on Maramba Boulevard, promotes the native language and culture, enhancing linguistic pride among Pangasinenses.

Daniel Maramba Monument

Don Daniel Bautista Maramba, known as the “Grand Old Man of Pangasinan,” served as the Municipal President of Sta. Barbara and the seventh Governor of Pangasinan. His monument, unveiled in 2007 on Maramba Boulevard, commemorates his contributions to the province, including the construction of the Provincial Capitol Building.

ASNA Monument

The ASNA Monument in Lingayen, first unveiled in 2011 and later relocated beside the Veterans Memorial Park, honors outstanding Pangasinenses. Designed by Ernesto V. Elduayan, this monument features fiber resin cubes symbolizing salt, Pangasinan’s main product, and recognizes excellence in various fields such as government, arts, medicine, and education.

Our Lady of Immaculate Conception Cathedral

The Our Lady of Immaculate Conception Cathedral in Pangasinan features a bell tower and stained glass windows that illuminate its interior. The well-maintained compound and the central altar with gold pillars enhance its spiritual and architectural beauty.

Bangol Craggs

The Bangol Craggs offer a picturesque view of the sea, making it a popular spot for visitors to watch sunsets and enjoy the natural beauty of Pangasinan’s coastline.

St. Catherine of Alexandria Parish Church

Constructed in 1834, the St. Catherine of Alexandria Parish Church is one of the oldest churches in Pangasinan. Its facade features an image of St. Catherine, and the interior includes a two-tiered retablo and stained glass windows depicting the life of Jesus Christ. An adjacent convent, built in 1845, adds to its historical significance.
