Dagupan City

Dagupan City was initially called Bacnotan by the Augustinian missionaries shortly after the establishment of the Spanish encomienda in Pangasinan in 1583. In 1660, local chieftain Andres Malong led a revolt against the Spaniards due to the imposition of forced labor. Following the rebellion, the town was rebuilt and renamed "Nandaragupan," meaning "where people meet," to commemorate the gathering of forces under Malong. In 1780, the name was simplified to Dagupan. Spanish rule ended on July 23, 1898, when Spanish troops surrendered to Katipunan leader General Francisco Makabulos.
