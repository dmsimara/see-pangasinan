Name Etymology

The name for Pangasinan was coined through the word “panag asinan,” which was further derived from the prefix “pang-,” which has the same use as the word “for,” and the root word “asin” meaning “salt.” With this, the name “Pangasinan” essentially means “place of salt” or “place of salt-making.”

Overview

Pangasinan, located in western Luzon, is a vibrant province known for its rich cultural heritage and scenic landscapes, home to over 3 million residents. Its economy thrives on agriculture, particularly rice, corn, and fish farming, with tourism also playing a significant role thanks to attractions like the Hundred Islands National Park and Bolinao Falls. The provincial capital, Lingayen, and cities like Dagupan serve as hubs of commerce and culture. Infrastructure development has improved connectivity and quality of life, with advancements in education, healthcare, and recreation. Culturally, Pangasinan celebrates its diverse traditions through festivals like the Bangus Festival and Pistay Dayat, showcasing the province's vibrant spirit.
