Notable Figures from Pangasinan

Juan Tamad

A legendary figure in Philippine folklore, Juan Tamad is known for his extreme laziness and often foolish antics. However, his simple solutions, despite their silliness, sometimes proved surprisingly effective. Though considered a trickster, there can be hidden wisdom in his unconventional approaches.

Angela “Angel” Perez Baraquio

A renowned folk singer and a passionate advocate for Pangasinan's rich cultural heritage. Baraquio's powerful vocals and dedication to preserving the unique musical traditions of Pangasinan have earned her national recognition. She plays a vital role in keeping Pangasinan's cultural identity alive for future generations.

Rogelio Sicat

Rogelio Sicat was a highly acclaimed fictionist, essayist, and poet from Pangasinan. He was a versatile writer who expressed himself fluently in both English and Pangasinan languages. His works garnered numerous awards and recognition, including the prestigious designation as a National Artist for Literature, though the specific year of his conferment isn't readily available online.

Victorio Edades

A celebrated National Artist of the Philippines for the Visual Arts (Painting). Born in Pangasinan, Edades is considered a pioneer of the modernist art movement in the Philippines. He challenged traditional art forms with his expressive and vibrant paintings, often incorporating social commentary and capturing the essence of Filipino life. His legacy continues to inspire contemporary Filipino artists.

Oscar Cruz

A prominent Filipino Catholic leader who served as the Archbishop of Lingayen-Dagupan from 1991 to 2009. Beyond his administrative duties, he was widely recognized for his outspoken advocacy within the Church, particularly on issues of social justice.

Dagupan Archbishop Emeritus Socrates Villegas

He led the Catholic Bishops' Conference of the Philippines (CBCP) as President from 2013 to 2017. This position placed him at the forefront of the Catholic Church in the Philippines for a significant period. You can explore further details about his accomplishments or stances during his presidency to learn more about his impact.

Juan dela Cruz Palaris

(Also known as Pantaleon Perez) - Lived during the Spanish colonial era and is remembered for leading the Palaris Revolt in 1762. This uprising challenged Spanish rule and highlighted the desire for freedom among the Pangasinan people.

Corazon “Cory” Cojuangco Aquino

Corazon “Cory” Cojuangco Aquino was the first female president of the Philippines, serving from 1986 to 1992. She played a pivotal role in the People Power Revolution, a peaceful uprising that ended the two-decade dictatorship of Ferdinand Marcos. This revolution is considered a significant event in democratic history.

Fidel V. Ramos

The 12th president of the Philippines (1992-1998). Born in Lingayen, Pangasinan, he was a decorated military general and played a key role in the People Power Revolution that toppled Ferdinand Marcos.

Urduja

A legendary warrior princess, Urduja is a central figure in Pangasinan folklore. While historical evidence for her existence is debated, she remains a powerful symbol of the bravery and strength associated with Pangasinan's people.
