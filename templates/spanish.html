Spanish Colonial Period

1571 Martin de Goiti conquers and colonizes the area.
April 5, 1572: Pangasinan becomes an encomienda, bringing it under Spanish jurisdiction.
1572: Juan de Salcedo explores northern Luzon, including Pangasinan.
1574-1575: Chinese corsair Limahong establishes a kingdom in Pangasinan but is pursued and driven out by Juan de Salcedo.
April 5, 1580: Pangasinan is established as an alcaldia mayor (province) by Governor General Gonzalo Ronquillo de Pe√±alosa with Don Pedro Manrique as its first alcalde mayor.
1611: The territorial boundaries of Pangasinan are officially defined, consolidating it as a functional political subdivision.
1660: The Malong Revolt, led by Andres Malong, takes place against Spanish rule.
1762: The Palaris Revolt, led by Juan dela Cruz Palaris, breaks out due to resentment over Spanish tributes but ends with the capture of Palaris.
1840: The Casa Real, a significant administrative and judicial building, is constructed in Lingayen.
1855: Sual is designated as an official port for foreign trade.
