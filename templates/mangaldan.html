Mangaldan

Mangaldan, one of the oldest towns in Pangasinan, was founded in June 1600. There are three stories about how it got its name. The first suggests that a Spanish missionary asked a local for the name of the place in Spanish. The local, thinking he was asking for water, responded with "manga-alay-adan," meaning "Adan is fetching." The second version claims that the town was named after its first native chief, Babaldan. The third story involves a large mango tree in the plaza where locals quarreled over its fruits, with the quarrel referred to as "man-nga-ngal-ngalan" in the dialect. Any of these versions could explain the town's name origin.
