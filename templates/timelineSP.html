Historical Timeline of Pangasinan

Pre-Colonial Period

8th Century A.D.

Ancient Malayo-Polynesians of Austronesian establish settlements along Lingayen Gulf, naming Pangasinan and Caboloan.

Spanish Colonial Period

1571

D. Martin de Goiti conquers and colonizes the area.

April 5, 1572

Pangasinan is made an encomienda, bringing it under Spanish jurisdiction.

1572

Juan de Salcedo explores northern Luzon, including Pangasinan.

1574-1575

Chinese corsair Limahong establishes a kingdom in Pangasinan but is pursued and driven out by Juan de Salcedo.

April 5, 1580

Pangasinan is established as an alcaldia mayor by Governor General de Pe√±alosa with Don Manrique as its first alcalde mayor.

1611

The territorial boundaries of Pangasinan are officially defined, consolidating it as a functional political subdivision.

1660

The Malong Revolt, led by Andres Malong, takes place against Spanish rule.

1762

The Palaris Revolt, led by Juan dela Cruz Palaris, breaks out due to resentment over Spanish tributes but ends with the capture of Palaris.

1840

The Casa Real, a significant administrative and judicial building, is constructed in Lingayen.

1855

Sual is designated as an official port for foreign trade.

Philippine Revolution and American Period

December 27, 1897

General Emilio Aguinaldo is exiled to Hong Kong via Sual following the Pact of Biak-na-Bato.

July 22, 1898

A Katipunan board is established in Pangasinan, leading to the defeat of the remaining Spanish forces in Dagupan.

February 5, 1899

President Emilio Aguinaldo relocates the provincial capital to San Carlos due to the Philippine-American War.

November 20, 1899

American forces complete the conquest of Pangasinan.

February 16, 1901

The Taft Commission establishes Pangasinan as a civil province with Lingayen as the capital.

Japanese Occupation and World War II

December 22, 1941

Japanese forces invade Pangasinan during World War II.

January 9-13, 1945

Allied Forces land in Lingayen and begin the liberation of Pangasinan.

February 1945

The provincial government was restored with Sofronio Quimson as governor and Dagupan temporarily as the capital.

Post-War Period

1953

Construction of the Princess Urduja Palace begins.

June 30, 1992

Fidel V. Ramos, a Pangasinense, becomes President of the Philippines.

1998

Construction of the San Roque Multipurpose Project begins.

1999

The Sual Coal-Fired Power Plant begins operations.
