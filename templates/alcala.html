Alcala

Originally known as "Dangla," a thriving barrio of Bayambang, Alcala became an official municipality on September 20, 1875, through Royal Decree No. 682. It was named Alcala after a town in Spain, the birthplace of the then-Governor of Pangasinan. The town's early settlers were adventurous, sturdy, diligent, industrious, and thrifty people from the Ilocos Provinces who traveled mainly on animal-drawn carts.
